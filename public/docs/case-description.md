# Case description — Hotel le Bonheur

*Bundled source document for SIMCESIM. The narrative behind the levers — why winter is
soft, why dividends matter, and who is judging performance. Feeds the explain layer and
(v2) the chatbot corpus.*

---

## The hotel

Hotel le Bonheur is a **family hotel with 50 rooms**. Demand is predominantly
**leisure-driven**, with a smaller base of **business travelers in winter**. The bulk of
the year's profit is made in the **summer high season**; the winter goal is more modest —
**avoid losses** and keep the operation healthy until demand returns.

Each season runs **180 nights**, so the hotel's capacity is **50 × 180 = 9,000
room-nights per season**. A room-night that goes unsold is gone forever — hotel inventory
is perishable, which is why occupancy and filling rooms matter so much.

## The owner

The hotel is owned by the former Mrs. Charriott, who **expects a steady stream of
dividends**. Profit alone isn't the whole picture: the owner wants cash returned. This is
why the dividend decision carries weight — but dividends can only be paid out of
**available retained earnings**, never borrowed.

## The market

The shares are **thinly traded over-the-counter**, so the share price is sensitive and
not always a smooth signal. **Competition is intensifying** — rivals (The Northline,
Blue, Hotel of America) are contesting the same guests, which is part of why the demand
you actually realize depends on their actions as well as yours.

## Management

The **previous management team was fired**. You are the new team, and your performance is
judged on the company's results — ultimately on **Cumulative Total Shareholder Return
(TSR)**: share-price change plus dividends, reinvested and annualized.

---

## What this means for your decisions

- **Seasonality is real.** Aim to make the money in summer; in winter, protect the
  downside. Pricing and marketing should reflect which guests you're chasing — leisure in
  summer, the steadier business traveler in winter.
- **The owner watches dividends.** A consistent payout matters, but only from earnings.
- **Quality and reputation compound.** Maintenance and personnel quality feed the Quality
  Level that supports both demand and the rate you can charge.
- **Competition is live.** The exact demand you'll realize against rivals is not knowable
  in advance — which is the honest boundary SIMCESIM respects. Use the levers and their
  directions; confirm with the Projections; learn the model by playing it.
